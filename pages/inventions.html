ANALYTICAL ENGINE

Charles Babbage

Invented on 1834

The Analytical Engine has many essential features found in the modern digital computer. It was programmable using punched cards, an idea borrowed from the Jacquard loom used for weaving complex patterns in textiles.

TELEPHONE

Alexander Graham Bell

Invented on 1876–1877

Telephones are used to transmit voice conversations, allowing people to talk to each other when they're not all in the same place. Telephone conversations can be between two people, which is the most common case, or between multiple people at once. The telephone was invented in 1876 by Alexander Graham Bell.

KODAK

George Eastman

Introduced on 1888

The first Kodak camera, invented by George Eastman, was placed on the market in 1888. It was a simple handheld box camera containing a 100-exposure roll of film that used paper negatives instead of glass plate.

LIGHT BULB

Thomas Edison

Demonstrated on 1879

Edison had built his first high resistance, incandescent electric light. It worked by passing electricity through a thin platinum filament in the glass vacuum bulb, which delayed the filament from melting. Still, the lamp only burned for a few short hours.

PHONOGRAPH

Thomas Edison

Invented on 1877

The phonograph was invented by Thomas Alva Edison in 1877, whilst working on the telephone and the telegraph. You would speak into a mouthpiece and the vibrations would be recorded onto the tin cylinder by a recording needle which created grooves on the tin.

ELECTRONIC TELEVISION

Philo Farnsworth

Demonstrated on 1927

The first all-electronic television system was invented by Philo Farnsworth. His system used an “image dissector” camera, which made possible a greater image-scanning speed than had previously been achieved with mechanical televisions.

TABULATING MACHINE

Herman Hollerith

Invented on 1890

The tabulating machine was an electromechanical machine designed to assist in summarizing information stored on punched cards. Invented by Herman Hollerith, the machine was developed to help process data for the 1890 U.S. Census.

APPLE I

Steve Jobs

Invented on 1976

Steve Jobs was driven by his desire to create great products that he would enjoy using himself rather than products that would just bring profit to Apple. Every time a new product or software was released by Apple, it became better and better at helping people enjoy using digital technology.

TESLA COIL

Nikola Tesla

Invented on 1891

Tesla coil, an electrical transformer that uses high-frequency alternating current (AC) to increase voltage. Because of its extremely high voltage, the electricity in a Tesla coil can travel through the air, powering—or damaging—nearby electronic devices, often with arcs of lightninglike electricity.

HYDROELECTRIC POWER

Nikola Tesla

Built on 1895

Hydropower utilizes turbines and generators to convert that kinetic energy into electricity, which is then fed into the electrical grid to power homes, businesses, and industries.

COTTON GIN

Eli Whitney

Created on 1794

A modern mechanical cotton gin was created by American inventor Eli Whitney in 1793 and patented in 1794. Whitney's gin used a combination of a wire screen and small wire hooks to pull the cotton through, while brushes continuously removed the loose cotton lint to prevent jams.

COTTON

Eli Whitney

Created on 1794

The gin separated the sticky seeds from the fibers in short-staple cotton, which was easy to grow in the deep South but difficult to process. The gin improved the separation of the seeds and fibers but the cotton still needed to be picked by hand.
